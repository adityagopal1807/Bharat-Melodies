BIHAR
Folk songs of Bihar, reflect the essence of rural life, conveying tales of love, longing, and resilience. Rooted in the state's rich cultural heritage, these songs are often accompanied by traditional instruments like the dholak and tabla. Passed down through generations, Bihar's folk music continues to serve as a vibrant expression of its people's joys, struggles, and tradition.

Some of the popular Folk song of Bihar

  • BIDESIA
    •
"Bidesia" is a quintessential folk song genre hailing from Bihar, India, characterized by its emotive storytelling and melodic simplicity. "Bidesia" narrates the poignant tales of migration, typically of men leaving their homes in search of work. The lyrics, often infused with themes of love, longing, and homesickness, resonate deeply with the experiences of migrant workers and their families. "Bidesia" continues to serve as a poignant reminder of the struggles and aspirations of those who leave their roots behind in pursuit of a better life.

  • JHIJHIYA
    •
Jhijhiya is a vibrant folk song tradition originating from Bihar, India, deeply rooted in the region's cultural heritage. Typically performed during the auspicious festival of Holi, Jhijhiya songs celebrate the playful spirit of the festival, often narrating tales of love, romance, and the joyous camaraderie among people. Accompanied by rhythmic beats of traditional instruments like dholak, manjira, and dhol, these songs enliven the festive atmosphere, creating a sense of unity and merriment among communities. Jhijhiya folk songs not only entertain but also serve as a means of preserving Bihar's rich cultural legacy, passed down through generations with pride and enthusiasm.
